Apple typically takes 24–48 hours to approve a new developer account, but it can sometimes take up to a week. This is the step that most often extends timelines, so we strongly recommend setting it up as soon as possible — ideally the same day as your onboarding meeting.
The Apple Developer Program membership ($99/yr) is required to publish any app on the App Store. It's paid directly to Apple and covers your right to distribute apps under your own name or business. This is separate from anything you pay HomeStack — it's your account, your app.
Minor changes (like small color or keyword adjustments) can usually be accommodated if caught early. However, once we've submitted your customizations to the backend system, significant design changes may delay your timeline. Reach out to your onboarding specialist as soon as possible if something needs updating.
Once your app is live on the App Store and your onboarding is complete, you'll be introduced to your dedicated account manager. They'll help you get the most out of your app — from managing listings, to supporting your real estate clients, to handling any ongoing updates or changes.

Here's how to reach Apple's developer support directly:

  1. Go to developer.apple.com/login and sign in with your Apple ID and password — the same one you use on your iPhone.
  2. Scroll to the bottom of the page and click Contact Us (it's small, in white text).
  3. Select the box titled Membership and Accounts.
  4. Choose the button titled Program Enrollment.
  5. Click the phone icon and confirm your number — Apple will call you back directly.
